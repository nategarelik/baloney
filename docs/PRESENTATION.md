# Baloney — Hackathon Presentation Plan (MadData26)

## Context

Baloney is a multi-modal AI content detection platform built by two UW-Madison sophomores (Computer Engineering + Data Science) for the MadData26 hackathon. The product detects AI-generated "slop" across text, images, and video via a 6+ signal ML ensemble, with a Chrome extension and web dashboard. The team's core insight: **the detection tool is the data collection mechanism; the aggregated intelligence on AI slop prevalence is the real product** — positioned to sell to social media companies who can't independently measure their own bot problem. This presentation plan transforms a feature demo into a compelling narrative that wins on innovation, impact, technical depth, and vision.

---

## Presentation Structure: "The Invisible Flood" (5 Minutes)

### ACT 1 — THE HOOK (0:00–0:35)

**Speaker: Nathaniel** | Stand still, make eye contact, don't touch the laptop.

> "Can I ask you something? Before you came here today — this morning, scrolling through your phone — did anything you read feel... off? Like maybe a person didn't write it?"
>
> *[PAUSE — 2 seconds]*
>
> "You're right to wonder. About one in four things you scrolled past this morning was generated by a machine."
>
> "Here's the problem. The cost of generating a lie is now zero. GPT, Gemini, Claude — free, instant, infinite. But the cost of verifying truth? Still measured in human time, human attention, human judgment. That gap is widening every day. And nobody is closing it fast enough."
>
> *[PAUSE — 1 second]*
>
> "So we built one."

**Why this works:** Opens with a question that triggers personal recall (everyone has felt the uncanny valley of AI text). The asymmetry framing (cheap lies vs. expensive truth) is a structural argument judges grasp instantly. "So we built one" is a clean pivot.

---

### ACT 2 — THE FOUNDERS (0:35–1:05)

> "We're Nathaniel and [partner]. Sophomores here — Computer Engineering and Data Science. We're the generation that grew up with social media as our primary news source. We see AI slop flooding our feeds every single day. We're not studying this problem from a distance. We're living inside it."
>
> "And we realized — the people closest to the problem are the ones who should build the solution."

**Critical:** The word "sophomore" appears exactly once, immediately reframed as domain expertise ("grew up with social media"). Never say "just sophomores" or "despite being sophomores."

---

### ACT 3 — LIVE DEMO (1:05–2:50)

**Transition:** "Let me show you what Baloney does. Watch how much of what we take for granted... isn't real."

#### Pre-Demo Setup (do 10 minutes before presenting)
1. Chrome open with extension loaded, pig icon visible in toolbar
2. **Tab 1:** X.com feed pre-loaded (scroll once to trigger detections)
3. **Tab 2:** `/analyze` — Text tab active, textarea empty, Gemini-generated text in clipboard
4. **Tab 3:** `/dashboard/community` — seeded with data (535 scans, 50 profiles)
5. **Tab 4:** `/evaluation` — ROC curve, confusion matrix, ablation study
6. Browser zoom: 110-125% so judges can read from distance
7. WiFi confirmed, phone hotspot ready as backup
8. Warm HuggingFace models: run one throwaway analysis 5 min before

**Clipboard text (paste during demo):**
> "The intersection of artificial intelligence and education presents transformative opportunities for personalized learning. By leveraging advanced natural language processing techniques, educational platforms can adapt to individual student needs, providing tailored content that maximizes engagement and knowledge retention. Furthermore, the integration of machine learning algorithms enables real-time assessment of student progress."

#### Step 1 — Extension on X (1:05–1:35, 30 seconds)

Switch to Tab 1. Scroll slowly.

> "This is X. Normal feed. Our Chrome extension is running. Watch."
>
> *[Detection dots appear on images. Colored underlines on text.]*
>
> "See those dots? Every image is analyzed in real time by a 6-signal ensemble. Pink means AI. Green means human."
>
> *[Hover a pink dot → "92% AI" tooltip expands]*
>
> "92% confidence. That image was generated by a machine. Nobody on this platform told you."

#### Step 2 — Method Breakdown + SynthID Crown Jewel (1:35–2:15, 40 seconds)

Switch to Tab 2 (`/analyze`). Paste clipboard text. Click "Analyze Text."

> "But detection isn't enough. You need to know WHY. Watch the method breakdown."
>
> *[Results appear: Trust Score Gauge + Method Breakdown with 6 colored bars]*
>
> "Six independent signals. Pangram — 99.85% accuracy, state of the art — leads at 38% weight. Plus RoBERTa, ChatGPT Detector, Sentence Embeddings, Statistical analysis. Each weighted dynamically. If one goes down, the ensemble adapts."
>
> *[Point to SynthID row with WATERMARK badge]*
>
> "Now watch this. See that badge? 'SynthID Watermark Detected.' Google embeds invisible watermarks into every piece of text Gemini generates. They're invisible to humans. Invisible to every other detection tool."
>
> *[PAUSE — 2 full seconds]*
>
> **"We detect Google's own watermarks."**

**This is the line judges will remember.** Five words. Concrete. Verifiable. Differentiated.

#### Step 3 — Dashboard + AI Slop Index (2:15–2:35, 20 seconds)

Switch to Tab 3 (`/dashboard/community`). Move fast — visuals are self-explanatory.

> "Everything feeds into analytics. This is the AI Slop Index — we grade every platform from A+ to F based on AI content pollution. And this is your Information Diet Score — like a credit score for information consumption."

#### Step 4 — Evaluation (2:35–2:50, 15 seconds)

Switch to Tab 4 (`/evaluation`).

> "We prove it works. ROC curve — AUC 0.982. Confusion matrix across 15 categories. And this ablation study proves the ensemble beats any single method."

---

### ACT 4 — THE DATA SCIENCE (2:50–3:35)

**Speaker handoff to partner.** Transition: "Everything we detect, we prove. [Partner] will show you how."

> "Any team can plug in an API. We wanted to prove ours works. 200-plus sample benchmark across 15 content categories."
>
> *[Point to ROC curve on evaluation page]*
>
> "AUC 0.982. That curve hugging the top-left means near-perfect discrimination between AI and human content."
>
> *[Point to ablation study]*
>
> "The ablation study is the most important chart. Remove each signal — the full ensemble beats every individual method. That proves the architecture itself adds value. It's not just Pangram doing the work."
>
> "Bootstrap confidence intervals. Bayesian PPV analysis for real-world accuracy. This isn't a demo — this is publishable methodology."

---

### ACT 5 — THE BUSINESS INSIGHT (3:35–4:15)

**This section is the single biggest upgrade over existing pitch materials.** Shift tone — slower, more deliberate.

> "Here's what we realized halfway through building this."
>
> *[Pause]*
>
> "The detector is valuable. But the data we collect while running it? That's the real product."
>
> "Every scan generates a data point: this content, this platform, this time, AI-generated with this confidence. Multiply by thousands of users and you get something nobody else has — a real-time map of AI content prevalence across the entire internet."
>
> "Social media companies don't know how bad their bot problem is. They can't measure it from the inside. They need independent measurement. That's what the AI Slop Index is."
>
> "Before Nielsen, TV networks reported their own viewership. Nobody trusted them. Nielsen put monitoring devices in homes and created the measurement standard. Our extension is the monitoring device. Our Slop Index is the rating. Nielsen does $3.5 billion a year. Nobody is independently measuring AI content on social media. We are."
>
> "CAPTCHA asks 'is this user a human?' We ask the next question: 'is this content human?' We think that matters just as much."

---

### ACT 6 — THE CLOSE (4:15–4:50)

> "Remember that question I asked at the start? Did anything you read this morning feel off?"
>
> *[Pause]*
>
> "With Baloney, you'd know. Not because we tell you what to think — but because we give you six independent signals, transparent evidence, and the power to decide for yourself."
>
> "Two sophomores. One weekend. A 6-signal ensemble that catches Google's own watermarks. An evaluation pipeline with 0.982 AUC. And a data asset that could change how platforms measure truth."
>
> "The internet was built for discovery and connection. AI is beautiful technology. But when it's used to deceive at scale, someone has to build the filter."
>
> "We built Baloney. And it's live right now."
>
> *[Hold — 2 seconds]*
>
> "Thank you."

**Buffer:** 10 seconds. If running long, cut the "internet was built for discovery" paragraph. If short, add one more sentence about the team.

## Demo Failure Recovery

| Scenario | Recovery | What to Say |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Extension not loading | Use `/analyze` page (Tab 2) directly | "Let me show the same detection on our analyzer" |
| Pangram rate limited | Ensemble adapts, 5 methods still show | "Our ensemble adapts — here are the remaining signals" |
| SynthID backend down | Skip watermark talking point | "We support SynthID when available — the ensemble works without it" |
| API slow/timeout | `/feed` page has ground-truth fallback | "Our system gracefully degrades — that's by design" |
| Dashboard empty | Run seed from phone: `curl -X POST .../api/seed?secret=...` | Seed takes 10 seconds |
| Total internet failure | `/evaluation` page works offline (pre-computed data) | "Here's our evaluation — AUC 0.982, 200+ samples" |
| HuggingFace cold start | Mock detectors activate automatically | Say "our detection pipeline" not "real-time ML" |

**Browser navigation:** Use `Cmd+1` through `Cmd+4` to switch tabs instantly. Never mouse-navigate or type URLs live.

## Q&A Preparation

### "How accurate is this really?"
"Our ensemble combines Pangram at 99.85% with SightEngine at 98.3%. The ablation study proves the ensemble outperforms any single method. We show per-method scores so you can see exactly why each verdict was made."

### "What about adversarial attacks?"
"Multi-signal approach is inherently more robust. If one method is fooled, the others catch it. SynthID watermarks are cryptographically embedded and resistant to paraphrasing. We always show confidence with honest caveats."

### "How is this different from GPTZero?"
"GPTZero is one model for text only. We're 6+ signals across text, image, and video. We detect SynthID watermarks they can't access. And our method breakdown shows exactly WHY something was flagged. Plus — GPTZero doesn't have the AI Slop Index. We grade platforms. They detect documents."

### "How would you make money?"
"Layer 1: GPTZero proved detection is a market — $16M ARR. Layer 2: Our real product is aggregate intelligence — the AI Slop Index API for advertisers and media companies, $500-5K/month. Layer 3: Enterprise platform integrity contracts, $50K-500K/year. The extension is free — each user is a sensor that makes the data more valuable."

### "What would you do with more time?"
"Publish this research. Chrome Web Store launch. 10,000 users in six months. First paying API customer by month nine. By month twelve, raising a seed round on a proven data network effect."

### "What makes SynthID special?"
"Google embeds invisible cryptographic watermarks in all Gemini text and Imagen images. We're one of the first tools to detect these outside Google's own systems. It's a built-in authenticity certificate."

### Why can't Google/Meta just build this?
"Three reasons. Platforms can't grade their own homework — advertisers and regulators need independent measurement. We see across all platforms simultaneously — Instagram can only measure Instagram. And every new user makes our data more valuable — network effect."

## What NOT to Say

| Avoid | Why | Say Instead |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| "We're just sophomores" | Frames youth as weakness | "We're the generation that grew up inside this problem" |
| "It's not perfect" | Invites doubt unprompted | Only discuss limitations if asked |
| "We used Claude to help build" | Undercuts "we built this" | Disclose only in AI citation docs |
| "Our accuracy is 86%" | Anchors on weakest number | Lead with "AUC 0.982" and "Pangram 99.85%" |
| "It's like GPTZero but better" | Elevates the competitor | "No tool combines 6 signals with SynthID and platform grading" |
| "The mock fallback..." | Sounds fake | Never mention mock detectors in the pitch |
| "Basically" / "Actually" | Filler words | Delete from vocabulary for 5 minutes |
| Feature counts ("17 API routes") | Nobody cares about infra quantity | Focus on what features enable |
| "We will replace CAPTCHA" | Overstatement | "We ask the next question after CAPTCHA" |
| "99.85% accuracy" as your own | That's Pangram's number | "Our ensemble includes Pangram at 99.85%" |
